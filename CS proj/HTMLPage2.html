Why is important and should be discussed?

Water is essential for life on Earth, serving as a fundamental building block for all living organisms. It plays a crucial role in various biological processes, such as digestion, circulation, and temperature regulation. For plants, water is vital for photosynthesis, enabling them to convert sunlight into energy, which in turn supports the entire food chain. Beyond its biological significance, water is also critical for maintaining ecosystems, as it shapes habitats and influences weather patterns. Additionally, it is indispensable for human activities, including agriculture, industry, and sanitation, making it a key driver of economic development. Access to clean and safe water is a cornerstone of public health, preventing diseases and promoting overall well-being. In a broader context, water serves as a cultural and spiritual symbol in many societies, reflecting its deep-rooted significance in human history and community identity. Therefore, the conservation and sustainable management of water resources are essential for ensuring a healthy planet and future generations.





Access to water, sanitation, and hygiene is a fundamental human right. To get back on track, key strategies include increasing sector-wide investment and capacity building, encouraging innovation and evidence-based action, improving cross-sectoral coordination and cooperation among all stakeholders, and taking a more integrated and holistic approach to water management. Water is critical not just for health, but also for poverty alleviation, food security, peace and human rights, ecosystems, and education. Nonetheless, countries confront increasing issues relating to water shortages, pollution, degraded water-associated ecosystems, and collaboration over transboundary water basins.
